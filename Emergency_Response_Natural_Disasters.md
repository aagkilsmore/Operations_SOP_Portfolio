# SOP: Natural Disaster Emergency Response & Recovery

## 1. Purpose & Objectives
The goal of this procedure is to establish a comprehensive framework for protecting personnel, assets, and the environment during natural disasters (e.g., Hurricanes, Earthquakes, Flooding). It outlines the transition from normal operations to emergency mode and the subsequent safe recovery of the facility.

## 2. Risk Context & Scope
This procedure applies to all regional facilities, including coastal terminals and inland storage sites. It accounts for specific site infrastructure, such as:
* **Marine Facilities:** Managing ship-to-shore line isolation and jetty security.
* **Bulk Storage:** Securing mounded and above-ground LPG storage vessels.
* **Production Plants:** Safe shutdown of chemical generation and compression units.

## 3. Emergency Response Phases

### 3.1 Phase I: Preparation & Alert (48-72 Hours Prior)
* **Communication:** Establish an Emergency Command Center and verify satellite/VHF radio functionality.
* **Site Securing:**
    * Lower and secure all crane booms and heavy equipment.
    * Inspect and clear all drainage systems and bunded areas to prevent flooding.
    * Secure all loose projectiles (cylinders, tools, and signage).
* **Inventory Management:** Top off all receiving tanks to maximum safe levels to increase weight/stability against high winds or storm surges.

### 3.2 Phase II: Active Response & Shutdown (0-24 Hours Prior)
1. **System Isolation:** Close all main storage tank valves and secure Emergency Shutdown (ESD) systems.
2. **Utilities:** Isolate electrical mains and gas supply lines to prevent secondary fires or leaks.
3. **Evacuation:** Conduct a final head-count and transition the site to "Unmanned/Secured" status as per local authority mandates.

### 3.3 Phase III: Post-Disaster Recovery
* **Damage Assessment:** Conduct a systematic site walkthrough once "All Clear" is given by authorities.
* **Integrity Verification:**
    * Perform leak tests on all high-pressure lines and storage vessels.
    * Check electrical systems for water damage before restoring power.
    * Verify the integrity of mounded tank shorelines and jetty structures.
* **Restart Protocol:** Re-commission the facility in stages, starting with critical safety systems before resuming production or transfer operations.

## 4. Continuity & Compliance
* **Documentation:** All incidents, damage reports, and recovery actions must be logged for insurance and regulatory review.
* **Audit Readiness:** This procedure is reviewed annually and updated following any major weather event to incorporate "Lessons Learned" into the Integrated Management System (IMS).
