# SOP: Ship-to-Shore LPG Transfer Operations

## 1. Purpose & Introduction
[cite_start]The purpose of this procedure is to provide standardized guidelines for conducting safe and reliable ship-to-shore transfer activities[cite: 32]. [cite_start]This document ensures that all operations align with international regulations and industry best practices to mitigate risk during high-pressure gas transfers[cite: 33].

This procedure is designed to meet the requirements of the following standards:
* [cite_start]**ISO 9001:2015** (Quality Management) 
* [cite_start]**ISO 45001:2018** (Occupational Health and Safety) 
* [cite_start]**ISO 14001:2015** (Environmental Management) 
* [cite_start]**NFPA 58** (Liquefied Petroleum Gas Code) 
* [cite_start]**SIGTTO** (Society of International Gas Tankers and Terminal Operators) 

## 3. Scope & Responsibilities
[cite_start]This SOP applies to all personnel and third-party contractors engaged in marine terminal transfer operations[cite: 130].

## 4. Operational Procedure

### 4.1 Pre-Arrival Preparation
* [cite_start]**Capacity Check:** Verify sufficient tank ullage for the incoming product quantity[cite: 155].
* [cite_start]**Safety Inspection:** Complete the pre-arrival checklist and inspect all LPG transfer hoses[cite: 156, 158].
* [cite_start]**Resource Mobilization:** Ensure VHF radios, fire extinguishers, and emergency PPE (life jackets, first aid) are staged and charged[cite: 161, 162, 168, 169].
* [cite_start]**Stakeholder Notification:** Inform surveyors, security, and port handlers at least 48 hours prior to ETA[cite: 170].

### 4.2 Vessel Arrival & Safety Meeting
1. [cite_start]**Mooring:** Verify the vessel is properly moored at the designated discharge point[cite: 177].
2. [cite_start]**Documentation:** Obtain the Notice of Readiness (NOR), Bill of Lading (BOL), and Certificate of Quality (COQ) [cite: 181-183].
3. [cite_start]**Safety Meeting:** Hold a pre-transfer meeting with the Chief Officer to agree on pumping rates, pressures, and emergency signals[cite: 185, 186, 189].
4. [cite_start]**Hose Connection:** Secure hoses between ship and shore, ensuring enough "free play" for tidal movements[cite: 190].

### 4.3 Discharge Operations
* [cite_start]**Pre-Flow Check:** Conduct a radio check and pressure test all connections using vapor before starting full flow[cite: 194, 197].
* [cite_start]**Flow Management:** Start pumping gradually at a low pressure, increasing to the agreed maximum after 20 minutes of stable flow[cite: 201].
* [cite_start]**Monitoring:** Perform hourly checks on tank levels, pressures, and the Emergency Shutdown (ESD) air compressor [cite: 203-206].
* **Tank Changeover:** Begin monitoring at 15-minute intervals as tanks approach 85% capacity. [cite_start]Transition flow to a new tank before closing the full tank's valves [cite: 207-209].

### 4.4 Completion & Disconnection
1. [cite_start]**Line Clearing:** Pump vapor through the lines for approximately 20 minutes to ensure no liquid LPG remains[cite: 238].
2. [cite_start]**Depressurization:** Bleed off pressure from hoses via the jetty elbow bleed valves[cite: 243].
3. [cite_start]**Secure Facility:** Disconnect hoses, fit end caps/blank flanges, and ensure all isolation valves are chained in the closed position [cite: 245-247].
4. [cite_start]**Final Reconciliation:** The Surveyor and Loading Master perform final tank calculations to resolve any volume discrepancies[cite: 249, 254].

## 5. Emergency Preparedness
* [cite_start]All personnel must be trained on the **Ship-to-Shore Emergency Response Plan**[cite: 257].
* [cite_start]Immediate shutdown is required via the ESD system in the event of a leak, fire, or communication loss[cite: 134].

## 6. Audit Readiness & Improvement
* [cite_start]**Self-Audits:** Conducted twice per year to validate equipment certification and operational conformance[cite: 259].
* [cite_start]**Lessons Learned:** Post-incident reviews are incorporated into this SOP to drive continual improvement[cite: 262].
