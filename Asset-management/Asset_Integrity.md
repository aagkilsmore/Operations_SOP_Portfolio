# SOP: Asset Integrity & Preventive Maintenance Management

## 1. Purpose & Objectives
The primary goal of this procedure is to ensure the mechanical integrity and reliability of all critical assets within the facility. This system is designed to prevent equipment failure, extend asset life cycles, and ensure compliance with global safety standards (PSM and ISO).

## 2. Framework & Compliance
This asset integrity program is built upon the following frameworks:
* **Process Safety Management (PSM):** Ensuring the integrity of "high-hazard" process equipment.
* **ISO 9001 & 45001:** Maintaining quality and safety through documented maintenance regimes.
* **Local Statutory Requirements:** Adhering to national pressure vessel and lifting equipment regulations.

## 3. Maintenance Strategy (CMMS Integration)
All assets are managed through the **eMaint Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS)**.

| Component | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Preventive Maintenance (PM)** | Scheduled intervals (Time-based or usage-based) to prevent failure. |
| **Corrective Maintenance** | Logged and tracked tasks to repair identified defects. |
| **Predictive Maintenance** | Condition monitoring (e.g., vibration analysis, thermography) where applicable. |

## 4. Operational Procedure

### 4.1 Asset Identification & Criticality
1. **Registry:** Every piece of equipment is assigned a unique Asset ID within the CMMS.
2. **Criticality Ranking:** Assets are ranked based on their impact on safety, environment, and production. High-criticality assets (e.g., LPG storage, ESD valves) receive priority scheduling.

### 4.2 The Maintenance Lifecycle
* **Scheduling:** The CMMS automatically generates Work Orders (WOs) based on pre-defined frequencies (Weekly, Monthly, Annual).
* **Execution:** Qualified technicians perform the tasks according to specific checklists attached to the WO.
* **Documentation:** Upon completion, technicians record findings, parts used, and time spent directly into the system.
* **Verification:** The Maintenance Manager or Supervisor reviews and "closes" the WO to ensure the standard was met.

### 4.3 Inspection & Testing (Mechanical Integrity)
* **Pressure Vessels:** Routine ultrasonic thickness (UT) testing and internal inspections per statutory timelines.
* **Safety Relief Valves:** Periodic bench testing and calibration to ensure activation at set pressures.
* **Piping & Valves:** Regular visual inspections and leak tests.

## 5. Performance Metrics & Reporting
To ensure continual improvement, the following Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are tracked:
* **PM Compliance:** Percentage of scheduled maintenance completed on time.
* **Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF):** Tracking reliability trends.
* **Maintenance Backlog:** Monitoring the volume of outstanding work.

## 6. Audit & Review
* **Internal Audits:** Conducted bi-annually to verify that physical equipment status matches CMMS records.
* **Management Review:** Maintenance data is presented during quarterly reviews to determine resource allocation and asset replacement needs.
