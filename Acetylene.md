# SOP: Acetylene Cylinder Filling & Plant Operations

## 1. Purpose & Introduction
The objective of this procedure is to establish a safe and efficient workflow for the production and filling of Acetylene cylinders. This process involves chemical reaction management, purification, and high-pressure compression, requiring strict adherence to safety protocols to prevent hazardous incidents.

## 2. Regulatory & Safety Standards
This operation is governed by:
* **ISO 9001:2015** (Quality Management)
* **ISO 45001:2018** (Occupational Health and Safety)
* **ISO 14001:2015** (Environmental Management)
* **Local Environmental Regulations** regarding chemical byproduct (lime slurry) disposal.

## 3. Operational Roles
| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Plant Operator** | Manages the generator, compressor, and chemical charging processes. |
| **Filler** | Handles cylinder connection, weight verification, and leak testing. |
| **Shift Supervisor** | Oversees safety compliance and production targets. |

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Pre-Operational Checks
* **Equipment Integrity:** Inspect the Acetylene generator, purifier, and compressor for any signs of gas leaks.
* **Safety Systems:** Verify that flashback arrestors are in place and that the fire suppression system is active.
* **Chemical Stock:** Ensure sufficient Calcium Carbide and Purifying Material are available for the production run.

### 4.2 Generator Operation & Gas Production
1. **Water Levels:** Confirm the generator water level is at the required operating mark.
2. **Carbide Charging:** Charge the Calcium Carbide hopper according to production requirements.
3. **Reaction Control:** Initiate the feed mechanism; monitor the reaction temperature to ensure it stays within safe operating limits.
4. **Purification:** Pass the raw gas through the scrubbing and purifying towers to remove impurities.

### 4.3 Cylinder Filling
* **Inspection:** Check cylinders for valid test dates and integrity; verify they have the correct quantity of solvent (acetone).
* **Connection:** Secure cylinders to the filling manifold and open valves.
* **Compression:** Start the compressor and monitor the inter-stage pressures.
* **Cooling:** Ensure the cooling water spray is operational to manage heat generated during compression.
* **Final Pressure:** Cease filling once the settled pressure reaches the approved maximum for the ambient temperature.

### 4.4 Post-Filling & Cleanup
1. **Leak Testing:** Conduct a soap-solution leak test on all cylinder valves before storage.
2. **Slurry Management:** Safely discharge the lime slurry byproduct into the designated pits for treatment/disposal.
3. **Documentation:** Record total gas produced, cylinders filled, and any equipment deviations in the shift log.

## 5. Emergency Response
* **Flashback:** If a flashback occurs, immediately close the cylinder valves and the main supply line.
* **High Temperature:** If the generator temperature exceeds the safety threshold, stop the carbide feed and increase water flow.

## 6. Maintenance & Compliance
* **Purifier Maintenance:** Regularly recharge purifying materials based on gas quality analysis.
* **Audit Readiness:** Maintain daily logs for temperature, pressure, and byproduct disposal for ISO verification.
